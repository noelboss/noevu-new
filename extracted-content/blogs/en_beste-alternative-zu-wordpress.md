### The WordPress reality: The "free" promise and the hidden costs

WordPress itself is free software. This fact is the platform's biggest marketing argument, but also its biggest misunderstanding. Running a professional, secure and powerful WordPress-based website is anything but free. The costs are hidden in a complex stack of necessary additional services and plugins that you have to purchase, install and maintain separately.

A realistic cost stack for a Swiss SME on WordPress looks like this:


*  **Hosting:** You need to choose a hosting provider such as Hostpoint, Cyon or Green.ch. The costs for reliable hosting in Switzerland start at around CHF 15-30 per month after the initial bait-and-switch offers.
  

*  **Premium theme/page builder:** While free themes are available, they often lack the flexibility and professional polish you need. For an appealing design and easy editing, a premium theme or page builder plugin like Elementor Pro (approx. $99/year) is almost essential.
  

*  **Security plugin:** This is not an option, but an absolute necessity. WordPress is the most hacked CMS in the world. A premium security plugin like Wordfence Premium (about $149/year) provides real-time protection that the free version, with its 30-day security update delays, cannot.
  

*  **Performance plugin:** Slow loading times are a conversion killer. To keep up with Squarespace's server-side optimization, you often need a caching plugin like WP Rocket (approx. $59/year).
  

*  **SEO plugin:** The best functions for professional search engine optimization can be found in premium plugins such as Yoast SEO Premium (approx. $99/year).
  

*  **Multilingualism:** To effectively serve the Swiss market with its several national languages, a plugin such as WPML (approx. $99/year) is required.
  

*  **Maintenance & time:** The biggest and most often overlooked cost factor. Every single one of these plugins, the theme and the WordPress core itself require regular updates. Each update carries the risk of compatibility issues that can cripple your site. This maintenance costs either your valuable time or the money of a developer who can quickly charge several hundred francs per hour.

### The WordPress reality: Security is your responsibility

!\[Squarespace security is an SME nightmare - or expensive.\](file:///Users/Noel\_1/Development/repos/noevu-new-2/extracted-content/old-site-backup/html/images/9d749094-2062-47c8-acbb-c4ce50ab683f/Squarespace+Sicherheit+ist+ein+pers%C3%B6nlicher+Alptraum)

Squarespace security is your personal SME nightmare - or expensive agency service.

With WordPress, the responsibility for security lies 100% with you, the website operator. As by far the most widely used content management system in the world, WordPress is a constant and primary target for hacker attacks. Without proactive and continuous measures, a WordPress website is an open barn door.

[Securing your WordPress site is an ongoing process that](https://woostify.com/wordpress-security-issues/) requires [technical knowledge and discipline](https://woostify.com/wordpress-security-issues/):


*  **Secure hosting:** Choosing a high-quality hoster with good security measures is the first step.
  

*  **Security plugins:** The installation and correct configuration of a robust security plugin such as Wordfence or Sucuri is essential.
  

*  **Regular updates:** You must always keep the WordPress core, your theme and *all* installed plugins up to date. Every outdated element is a potential security vulnerability.
  

*  **Strong passwords & user management:** Basic security hygiene is in your hands.
  

*  **Regular backups:** In the event that something does happen, up-to-date backups are your only life insurance.
  
From our own experience, we can say that a single omission in this chain can have catastrophic consequences: a hacked site, data theft from customers, reputational damage and high costs for having the site cleaned up by an expert. Particularly critical: free security plugins such as the basic version of Wordfence often only offer delayed protection. In the free versions, new threats are only included in the detection rules with a 30-day delay - a dangerous time window during which your site is unprotected.
